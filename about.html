Designing the Future of Hardware

I'm a graduate student at the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore, pursuing my M.Tech in Electronics Systems Engineering. My passion lies at the intersection of computer architecture, digital design, and VLSI — where elegant algorithms meet physical reality.

From implementing pipelined RISC-V processors to designing quantum key distribution receiver systems, I thrive on challenges that push the boundaries of what's possible in hardware. My work focuses on creating efficient, reliable, and innovative digital systems.

Currently, I'm working on a Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) Receiver System using the Coherent One-Way protocol, achieving 55ps timing resolution for secure optical communication.
